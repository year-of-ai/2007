## Notable Events of 2007

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Virginia Tech shooting](history-politics/virginia-tech-shooting.md) | On April 16, student Seung-Hui Cho killed 32 people at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, Virginia, making it the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history to that point. |
| [iPhone launched](science-technology/iphone-launch.md) | Apple released the first iPhone on June 29, combining phone, internet browser, and iPod in one device, transforming the smartphone industry and mobile computing. |
| [I-35W Bridge collapses](history-politics/i35w-bridge-collapse.md) | A steel truss bridge over the Mississippi River in Minneapolis collapsed on August 1, killing 13 people and injuring 145, prompting nationwide scrutiny of aging infrastructure. |
| [Benazir Bhutto assassinated](history-politics/benazir-bhutto-assassination.md) | Pakistan's former prime minister was shot and killed on December 27 in Rawalpindi after a political rally, destabilizing the country ahead of scheduled January elections. |
| [Hank Paulson Treasury crisis response](society-economics/hank-paulson-treasury-2007.md) | Treasury Secretary Paulson, former Goldman Sachs CEO, coordinated the government's early response to the subprime crisis, managing liquidity and international coordination through 2007. |
| [Ben Bernanke's Federal Reserve crisis response](society-economics/ben-bernanke-fed-2007.md) | Federal Reserve Chairman Bernanke, a Great Depression scholar, cut rates, created emergency lending facilities, and coordinated with central banks worldwide to prevent financial collapse in 2007. |
| [Subprime mortgage crisis erupts](society-economics/subprime-mortgage-crisis.md) | Bear Stearns disclosed in July that two of its subprime hedge funds had lost nearly all their value, marking the onset of the global financial crisis that deepened in 2008. |
| [*Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows* published](arts-culture/harry-potter-deathly-hallows.md) | J. K. Rowling's seventh and final Harry Potter novel was released on July 21, selling 11 million copies in the first 24 hours in the U.S. and UK. |
| [Al Gore and IPCC share Nobel Peace Prize](history-politics/al-gore-ipcc-nobel-peace-prize.md) | The Nobel Committee awarded the 2007 Peace Prize jointly to Al Gore and the IPCC on October 12 for their work building consensus on man-made climate change and its remedies. |
| [Amazon Kindle launched](science-technology/amazon-kindle-launch.md) | Amazon introduced the Kindle e-reader on November 19 at $399, selling out within 5.5 hours and fundamentally changing how consumers purchase and read books. |
| [Writers Guild of America strike](arts-culture/writers-guild-strike.md) | WGA members began a strike on November 5 over compensation for internet-distributed content, halting U.S. television production for 100 days and reshaping residual-pay rules. |
| [*No Country for Old Men* released](arts-culture/no-country-for-old-men.md) | The Coen Brothers' adaptation of Cormac McCarthy's novel opened in November, winning four Academy Awards including Best Picture at the 80th ceremony in February 2008. |
| [Facebook Platform opens to developers](science-technology/facebook-platform.md) | In May, Facebook unveiled its developer platform, enabling third-party applications on the social network and accelerating its growth past MySpace in global traffic. |
| [YouTube rises as global media platform](science-technology/youtube-growth-2007.md) | Following its Google acquisition in late 2006, YouTube achieved 100+ million daily views and 160 million users by 2007, launched its creator-revenue Partner Program, and partnered with CNN for citizen-driven presidential debates, establishing the foundation for the creator economy. |
| [Oil prices spike to near $100 per barrel](society-economics/oil-price-spike-2007.md) | Crude oil reached $99.29 per barrel on November 21, the closest prices had ever come to $100, signaling a structural shift in global energy markets. |
| [Stock market peaks before 2008 crisis](society-economics/stock-market-peak-october-2007.md) | The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed at an all-time high of 14,164.53 on October 9, marking the zenith of the bull market before the global financial crisis. |
| [Kurt Vonnegut dies](people/kurt-vonnegut.md) | The acclaimed American author of *Slaughterhouse-Five* and humanist moral voice died on April 11 at age 84 from injuries sustained in a fall, ending one of postwar literature's most influential careers. |
| [Luciano Pavarotti dies](people/luciano-pavarotti.md) | The Italian tenor, widely regarded as the greatest operatic voice of the 20th century, died of pancreatic cancer on September 6 at his home in Modena, aged 71. |
| [Boris Yeltsin dies](people/boris-yeltsin.md) | Russia's first post-Soviet president died of congestive heart failure on April 23 in Moscow aged 76; President Putin declared a national day of mourning on the day of his funeral. |
| [Iraq War Troop Surge announced](history-politics/iraq-war-troop-surge.md) | President Bush announced deployment of 30,000 additional U.S. troops to Iraq on January 10, shifting to counterinsurgency doctrine under General David Petraeus and defining U.S. military strategy for 2007. |
| [Darfur conflict escalates internationally](history-politics/darfur-conflict-2007.md) | The humanitarian crisis in Sudan's Darfur region, by 2007 the worst in the world, reached a turning point with ICC prosecutions of Sudanese officials, U.S. genocide declaration, and the establishment of UNAMID peacekeeping mission—marking a shift toward international accountability despite ongoing conflict. |
| [Induced pluripotent stem cells created](science-technology/induced-pluripotent-stem-cells.md) | In November, Yamanaka and Thomson independently reprogrammed adult human cells into pluripotent stem cells using transcription factors, opening regenerative medicine without embryo use and founding modern personalized therapeutics. |
| [Arctic sea ice hits record low](science-technology/arctic-sea-ice-record-low.md) | Arctic sea ice extent reached 4.13 million km² on September 16, the lowest in the satellite era since 1979, opening the Northwest Passage and confirming accelerating climate change. |
| [IPCC Fourth Assessment Report released](science-technology/ipcc-fourth-assessment-report.md) | The IPCC released its most comprehensive climate report in 2007, concluding with 90% certainty that human greenhouse gases cause most global warming and projecting 1.1–6.4°C temperature rise by 2100 depending on emission scenarios. |
| [Britney Spears shaves her head](arts-culture/britney-spears-head-shaving-2007.md) | On February 16, Britney Spears shaved her head at a Los Angeles salon during a personal crisis and custody battle, symbolizing her resistance to loss of agency and becoming a defining moment in celebrity mental health discourse. |
| [Live Earth 2007](arts-culture/live-earth-2007.md) | On July 7, over 150 musical acts performed simultaneously across twelve venues on all seven continents to raise environmental awareness, reaching 19 million U.S. viewers and demonstrating entertainment's role in climate advocacy. |
| [Imus in the Morning cancelled](arts-culture/imus-in-the-morning-cancellation.md) | Don Imus's racial comments about the Rutgers women's basketball team on April 4 sparked civil rights backlash, leading to cancellation by CBS Radio on April 12, demonstrating media accountability for inflammatory speech. |
